## Business Structure in Washington State

Washington State makes it straightforward to establish a professional practice.

**Sole proprietorship:** The simplest and most common starting point. You operate under your own name, take personal liability, and file income on your personal tax return. Many practitioners begin here and graduate to an LLC once their practice is established.

**Washington State LLC:** Provides limited liability protection and a more formal professional identity. The filing fee is $200 with the Washington Secretary of State (sos.wa.gov). Annual renewal costs $60 per year. If you are branding under a business name, an LLC is a good structure from the start.

**No state income tax:** Washington has no personal state income tax, one of only a handful of states in this position. Your earnings from a pet bereavement practice are subject to federal income tax and self-employment tax only. Washington does have a Business and Occupation (B&O) tax on gross business receipts, but the rates are low and the threshold for small service businesses is generous. Most new practitioners will pay little or nothing in B&O tax in their first year.

**No license required:** Pet loss support is not a clinical mental health service. You are offering compassionate presence, structured conversation, and guided remembrance, not therapy or diagnosis. No Washington State license is required to offer these services.

## TRACE Certification: What It Is and What You Get

TRACE stands for Therapeutic Remembrance for Animal Companions and their Endings. It is a five-step framework grounded in Worden's Four Tasks of Mourning and Continuing Bonds Theory, two of the most widely respected bodies of grief research.

The five steps are:

1. **Tell the Story** -- the client shares the full arc of their relationship with the animal, from first meeting to final day.
2. **Recognise the Bond** -- the depth and significance of the human-animal connection is explicitly named and honoured.
3. **Acknowledge the Pain** -- grief is validated without minimization or rush.
4. **Celebrate the Life** -- the animal's personality, quirks, and presence in the household are fully honoured.
5. **Embrace What Remains** -- the client is supported in integrating the loss into an ongoing sense of connection, meaning, and identity.

Approximately 97% of a TRACE session is compassionate presence and structured conversation. No clinical background is required. The framework is designed for people from any professional background: veterinary staff, coaches, chaplains, social workers, animal care professionals, or caring individuals with no prior formal training.

## Getting Your First Clients in Seattle

Seattle's professional networks, strong animal welfare community, and culture of referral mean you have multiple practical routes to your first clients.

**Veterinary practices:** Introduce yourself to vet clinics in Capitol Hill, Fremont, Ballard, and Bellevue. Bring a simple one-page summary of what you offer. Veterinarians in Seattle frequently deal with clients facing euthanasia decisions or sudden loss and have nowhere to refer them. A practitioner they trust solves a real problem for them. One or two strong vet relationships can generate consistent client flow.

**Seattle Humane (Bellevue) and Seattle Animal Shelter:** Both organizations work with families navigating loss from multiple angles. Contact their community and volunteer teams with a brief introduction.

**PAWS (Progressive Animal Welfare Society, Lynnwood):** PAWS is a cornerstone of Washington's animal welfare community with a broad, values-aligned membership base. A relationship with PAWS can open doors to a network of animal-focused professionals and advocates who share your commitment to the human-animal bond.

**Pasado's Safe Haven:** A well-known Washington animal sanctuary with strong community ties and a following among Seattle's values-driven demographic.

**Pet cremation services:** Faithful Friends Pet Cremation and Washington Cremation both serve families in acute grief. A conversation with their teams and referral cards in their waiting areas is a low-effort, high-impact presence.

**Online community:** Seattle has active Reddit communities (r/seattledogs, r/seattle) and a strong Instagram presence among pet owners. Consistent, genuine participation in these communities builds visibility over time.
