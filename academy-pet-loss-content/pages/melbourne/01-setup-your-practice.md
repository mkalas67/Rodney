## Business Structure in Melbourne

Setting up your practice is administratively straightforward. Here is what you actually need to do.

**Register for an ABN.** An Australian Business Number is free and takes around ten minutes online at abr.gov.au. You need an ABN to invoice clients, register a business name, and receive payment professionally. Most sole traders operating as pet bereavement counsellors will operate as a sole trader from day one. You do not need to form a company.

**Register a business name (optional but recommended).** If you want to trade under a name other than your own, register a business name through ASIC at asic.gov.au. As of 2025, this costs approximately AUD $40 per year for a one-year registration, or AUD $94 for three years. A name like "Melbourne Pet Loss Support" or "Gentle Farewells Melbourne" immediately signals your specialisation.

**Pet loss counselling is not regulated by AHPRA.** This is important and often misunderstood. The Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA) regulates health professions including psychology, nursing, and physiotherapy. Pet bereavement counselling is not a regulated health profession. You do not need AHPRA registration, a supervised practice period, or a clinical qualification to work as a pet bereavement counsellor.

**Medicare and NDIS do not apply.** Pet loss support is not a Medicare-rebatable service and does not fall under NDIS funding. You will operate as a private-pay practitioner, which is standard for this niche.

**Professional indemnity insurance.** While not legally mandated, professional indemnity insurance is strongly advisable. Several Australian insurers offer policies for coaches, counsellors, and complementary therapists. Premiums for sole traders typically start around AUD $400-$600 per year.

**Record keeping.** Keep basic financial records (income, expenses, ABN details) for tax purposes. A simple spreadsheet works for most sole traders starting out. An accountant familiar with small service businesses can make this effortless.

## Credentials: What Actually Matters

Clients in Melbourne are sophisticated. They want to know you are qualified. The question is: qualified in what?

Because pet bereavement counselling is not a clinical service, there is no single prescribed qualification. What matters is demonstrating that you have a structured, evidence-informed framework for supporting grief, that you understand the specific nature of pet loss, and that you practise with professionalism and care.

A certificate from a recognised programme in pet loss support or animal bereavement counselling is your primary credential. It tells clients you have done the work, learned the framework, and are accountable to a professional standard.

General life coaching or counselling certificates can complement your pet loss credential but are not a substitute for it. Clients who find you specifically for pet loss support want someone who specialises.

## Getting Your First Clients in Melbourne

Your first clients will almost certainly come from your local network and from referral relationships you build with vet practices and animal services.

**Vet practices** are your most important referral source. Local practices in Fitzroy, Brunswick, St Kilda, Richmond, and Carlton see grief-stricken clients regularly, especially around end-of-life decisions and euthanasia. A brief, warm letter of introduction explaining your service and how you support clients after a loss, with a small stack of business cards or a flyer, is an effective opening. Follow up with a personal visit where possible.

**RSPCA Victoria and AWL Victoria** both work with bereaved pet owners. A relationship with staff and volunteers at these organisations can generate steady referrals, particularly around surrender and euthanasia cases.

**Lort Smith Animal Hospital** in North Melbourne is one of Australia's largest not-for-profit animal hospitals. Their staff encounter pet loss daily. An introduction to their client services or veterinary social work team is worthwhile.

**Pet cremation services** are an excellent referral partner. Pet's Rest Cremation Services and Victorian Pet Cremation Services work with families at the moment of greatest grief. A card or brochure with local pet cremation providers creates a natural handoff.

**Melbourne Dog Rescue** and similar adoption organisations often work with volunteers and adopters who have experienced pet loss. Community involvement with these groups raises your profile organically.

**The equestrian community** in Melbourne's outer suburbs (Belgrave, Lilydale, Healesville, Mornington Peninsula) is almost entirely without specialised grief support for horse loss. Reaching out to equestrian centres, farriers, and equine vets in these areas can open a niche with very low competition.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a psychology or counselling degree to practise as a pet bereavement counsellor in Melbourne?**
No. Pet bereavement counselling is not a regulated health profession under AHPRA. A certificate in pet loss support, combined with the TRACE framework, is sufficient to practise professionally.

**Is pet loss counselling covered by Medicare or private health insurance?**
No. Pet loss support is not a Medicare-rebatable service and is not typically covered by private health insurance extras. Clients pay out of pocket, which means your fees are set entirely by you.

**How long does it take to set up my practice?**
With an ABN registered, a basic website live, and your TRACE certification in progress, you can begin taking clients within four to six weeks. Many practitioners take their first client before completing certification, offering an introductory rate while they complete modules.

**Can I run my practice online?**
Yes. Online sessions work extremely well for pet bereavement support. Melbourne's professional population is highly comfortable with video calls, and an online practice removes all geographic limits.

**What if I have no prior experience with grief support?**
The TRACE programme is designed for people without clinical backgrounds. The framework provides a clear structure for every session, so you always know what you are doing and why.
