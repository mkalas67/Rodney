# How to Price Your Pet Loss Support Sessions in Birmingham

Getting your pricing right from the start is one of the most important business decisions you will make. Price too low and you undermine your credibility as well as your income. Price too high without the experience to justify it and you stall before you build momentum. This guide gives you concrete, Birmingham-specific figures, practical session structures, and a clear picture of what your income could look like.

---

## What the Birmingham Market Currently Looks Like

Birmingham is a large, economically diverse city. The areas with the highest concentration of pet owners who pay for professional services tend to be the more affluent suburbs: Moseley, Harborne, Edgbaston, Sutton Coldfield, and Solihull. These areas also have higher rates of private therapy use generally, which means potential clients are more familiar with paying for professional emotional support.

The city centre and Heartlands areas have strong pet ownership but a somewhat more price-sensitive market. Online delivery is valuable here because it removes the barrier of travelling to a consulting room and widens your accessible client base across the entire conurbation.

Based on current rates for non-clinical wellbeing practitioners and specialist support services in Birmingham, the following ranges apply:

**Early-career practitioners (newly certified, building a caseload):**
- Single session: £50-£65
- Three-session package: £135-£175
- Six-session package: £250-£320

**Established practitioners (12 months or more of practice, regular referral base):**
- Single session: £75-£100
- Three-session package: £200-£265
- Six-session package: £375-£500

These figures reflect a 50-60 minute session. If you are offering 75-90 minute extended sessions, you can add £15-£25 to each band.

Sessions in Birmingham generally sit slightly below Edinburgh and London rates but above rural England. The large professional population in Brindleyplace and surrounding business districts is comfortable with rates at the higher end of these ranges, particularly if you have a strong profile and visible credentials.

## Session Structures That Work

**Single session.** Best for clients who want to try the experience before committing, or for people in acute grief who need support now without thinking beyond the next appointment. Charge your standard rate. Single sessions are also useful for memorial occasions: an anniversary, a birthday, or the date of euthanasia.

**Three-session package.** The most commonly booked structure for new clients. It provides enough space to work through the TRACE framework properly (Tell the Story and Recognise the Bond tend to take the first session; the remaining three steps unfold over the following two). Packages should be paid in advance or by the second session at the latest. Discount of around 10% versus single session price.

**Six-session package.** Appropriate for more complex grief, multiple pet losses, or clients who appreciate a sustained container. A 15% discount from single-session rate is a reasonable incentive.

**Free introductory call.** Offer a 20-minute introductory call at no charge. This is not a free session; it is a brief, structured conversation where the client explains their situation and you explain your approach. It removes the financial risk of a first contact and dramatically increases conversion from enquiry to booked client. Almost all practitioners who use this structure report that the majority of introductory calls convert to paid bookings.

Do not charge for no-shows without a 24-hour cancellation policy in place. State your cancellation policy clearly at booking and in your confirmation email.

## When to Raise Your Prices

Raise your rates when:

- You have a consistent waiting list (even short) rather than immediate availability
- You have been practising for 12 months or more without a significant break
- You have positive client reviews or testimonials
- Your Academy directory listing shows strong engagement

Do not wait until you feel "ready." The impulse to stay at an early-career rate long past when it is warranted is extremely common and significantly limits income. A modest increase of £10-£15 per session is unlikely to lose existing clients and will feel appropriate to new ones.

Give existing clients at least four weeks' notice of a price increase and honour their current rate for any pre-booked sessions.

## Sliding Scale Considerations

Offering a small number of sliding scale spaces is entirely optional. If you choose to do so, set a floor (a minimum rate you will not go below, such as £35) and limit sliding scale availability to two or three spots in your caseload. State clearly that these spaces are limited and that the reduced rate is means-tested rather than a general discount.

Be cautious of sliding scale in Birmingham's larger demographic context. The city's diversity means you may encounter genuine financial hardship, but it also means making assumptions about who needs reduced fees based on postcode or cultural background. Let clients self-select by making the option available and letting them come to you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I charge the same for online and in-person sessions in Birmingham?**
Yes, in most cases. Your time and expertise are the same regardless of medium. Some practitioners charge a small travel supplement (£5-£10) for in-person sessions that require them to travel to a hired room. If you use your own home space for in-person work, keep rates equal.

**What is a fair price for a free introductory call?**
It is free. Do not charge for it. The introductory call is a marketing and conversion tool, not a session. Keep it to 20 minutes with a clear structure.

**How do I handle clients who ask for a discount?**
If you have sliding scale spaces available, you can offer one. Otherwise, be warm but clear: "My current rate is £X per session. I do hold a small number of reduced-rate spaces for those with genuine financial barriers; if that applies to you, let me know and we can discuss it." You do not need to justify your rate.

**Is it worth offering packages early on in my Birmingham practice?**
Yes. Packages provide cash flow predictability and improve client outcomes by creating a clear commitment to the process.

**When should I start charging at the established rate?**
After 12 months of regular practice, a portfolio of positive client experiences, and when your enquiry volume is strong enough that you sometimes have to tell people there is a short wait.
