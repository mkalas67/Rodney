If you are building a pet bereavement counselling practice here, your advertising strategy needs to reflect that reality. The channels that work best in Queensland are not always the same ones that dominate in Sydney or Melbourne, and knowing the difference will save you time and money.

This page covers every effective advertising channel for a pet bereavement practitioner in Brisbane, from free listings that generate traffic from day one through to paid campaigns when you are ready to grow.

## Google Business Profile

A Google Business Profile is free and generates local search visibility before your website has any organic ranking. It places you on Google Maps and in local search results for terms like "pet grief counsellor Brisbane" and "pet loss support near me."

Set up your profile at business.google.com. You do not need a physical office: select "service area business" and enter the Brisbane suburbs you serve. For a Brisbane-wide practice, you might list suburbs across the north, south, east, and west, plus the inner city. If you are also serving the Gold Coast corridor, include those suburbs.

Complete every section: business category (counsellor or mental health service as primary, with a description that specifies pet bereavement), hours, services, and a description that includes the phrase "pet loss counselling Brisbane."

Ask every satisfied client who is comfortable to leave a review. Four or five Google reviews in your first few months will place you above competitors who have none.

## Social Media: Brisbane's Most Effective Channels

**Facebook is the dominant platform in Queensland.** This is not an overstatement. Queensland has some of the most active Facebook community groups in Australia. Suburb-specific groups (Indooroopilly, Chermside, Carindale, Logan), pet owner groups, breed-specific groups, and community interest groups are where Brisbanites discuss local services, ask for recommendations, and share experiences.

You cannot advertise directly in most of these groups, but you can build a genuine presence. Join relevant groups. Offer helpful information when someone asks about pet grief. Share resources when the topic arises naturally. Do not spam, and do not make every comment a sales pitch. Over time, a reputation as a knowledgeable, caring practitioner in these groups generates direct enquiries and warm referrals.

A Facebook Business Page is essential. Brisbane clients of all ages use Facebook to search for local services. Your page should include your credentials, services, pricing, and a clear way to contact you or book. Post two to three times a week: a mix of grief support content, educational posts, and occasional warm personal content (relevant to your practice, not your personal life).

**Instagram** is the second platform worth maintaining in Brisbane, particularly for reaching the 25-40 demographic. Brisbane's lifestyle culture, strong outdoor identity, and growing wellness market make Instagram increasingly effective for complementary health practitioners. Short educational reels, carousel posts about the grief process, and warm visual content about pet-human bonds all perform well.

Use Brisbane location tags and hashtags including #petloss #petgrief #brisbanepets #petlossbrisbane #petbereavement #queenslandpets. Post consistently (three to four times per week) before investing in paid promotion.

**LinkedIn** is underused by pet bereavement counsellors everywhere, including Brisbane, and that is an advantage. Position yourself on LinkedIn as a specialist in pet grief support. Post occasional articles about the significance of animal companionship, the reality of pet grief in the workplace (employees who lose a pet and receive no acknowledgement from their employer), and the growing recognition of this niche in Australia.

Brisbane has a substantial corporate sector. Employee Assistance Programme providers in Queensland are a legitimate referral pathway, and LinkedIn is where those relationships form.

## Referral Marketing in Brisbane

Referral marketing is the process of building relationships with people and organisations who encounter bereaved pet owners before you do.

**Veterinary practices** are your most important referral source. Practices across Brisbane and along the Gold Coast corridor see end-of-life decisions, sudden deaths, and long-term illness every week. A professional introduction letter, followed by a personal visit to leave business cards or a brief brochure, is an effective approach. Speak to practice managers rather than front desk staff where possible. Frame your introduction around how you support their clients, not around selling your services.

**RSPCA Queensland** has operations across the state and works with bereaved pet owners, surrender cases, and end-of-life situations regularly. An introduction to community education or the Brisbane regional volunteer team is a worthwhile first step.

**Brisbane Animal Care and Protection Society (BACPS)** operates across Brisbane and encounters distressed pet owners regularly. Staff and volunteers there are a natural referral pathway.

**PetBarn Queensland** locations serve a large, loyal pet-owner community. Connecting with store community coordinators and managers raises awareness among a broad audience without requiring clinical referral relationships.

**Queensland Pet Cremation and Pawssum** both work with families at their most acute point of grief. A professional card or QR code card with these providers places you in front of people at exactly the moment they may be ready to seek support. Approach their client relations staff with a clear, brief introduction.

**Post-flood community groups.** The 2022 Brisbane floods left a lasting community awareness of sudden, traumatic loss, including pet loss. Facebook groups and community organisations that formed around the floods, as well as Queensland emergency services and SES volunteers who dealt with animal rescue and recovery during the disaster, are appropriate and sensitive points of outreach. Frame any introduction around your specific ability to support sudden, traumatic loss, and around the community's lived experience.

**Grief counsellors and psychologists** who do not specialise in pet loss will sometimes receive enquiries from clients for whom this is a primary issue. A brief introductory email to general counsellors in Brisbane explaining your service and when a referral is appropriate creates a mutually beneficial relationship.

## Content Marketing: Building Visibility in Queensland

Content marketing means creating useful, searchable content that brings people to you. For a Brisbane pet bereavement practitioner, this means answering the questions Queensland pet owners are actually asking.

Effective topics include: "Is it normal to grieve this much after a pet dies?", "How to help a child through the loss of a family pet," "Sudden pet loss versus anticipated loss: why they feel different," "Pet grief in the workplace: what employers should know," "How to support a friend who has lost a pet," and "What to expect after your pet's euthanasia appointment."

A blog with one well-written, 800-1,200 word post per month builds cumulative search visibility over twelve to eighteen months. Include Brisbane-specific terms and Queensland-specific references. Repurpose each post as a Facebook note, a LinkedIn article, and two or three Instagram captions. One piece of content becomes five touchpoints for minimal additional effort.

## Paid Advertising: Testing in Brisbane

Paid advertising is not necessary in your first few months, but it is a controllable, scalable growth lever when you are ready.

**Google Ads** targeting search terms like "pet loss counsellor Brisbane," "pet bereavement support Queensland," and "grief counselling after pet death" captures explicit intent. Click costs in this niche in Brisbane are typically lower than general counselling terms. AUD $200-$400 per month is a reasonable test budget. Track enquiries carefully: know which keywords generate contact, and pause the ones that do not.

**Facebook and Instagram Ads** allow precise targeting of Brisbane and Gold Coast residents by interests (pet owner, animal welfare, dog, cat, pet adoption) and by life events. A promoted post offering a free resource (a downloadable guide to pet grief, or a free 20-minute initial call) typically outperforms a direct "book a session" ad. Facebook ads in particular are very effective in Queensland given the platform's dominance here.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long until I see enquiries from advertising?**
Your Academy directory listing can generate enquiries within days of going live. A verified Google Business Profile typically begins appearing in local searches within one to three weeks. Organic social media builds over two to three months of consistent posting.

**Do I need a website before I advertise?**
You can launch with just the Academy directory listing and a Google Business Profile. A basic website adds credibility and gives you somewhere to direct traffic, but it is not required to take your first client.

**Is Facebook really the most important platform for Brisbane?**
For building community presence, reaching the over-35 demographic, and participating in the community groups where referrals originate, yes. Instagram is the stronger channel for the 25-40 cohort. Both are worth maintaining.

**Should I pay for Facebook ads straight away?**
Build organic content for two to three months first. Understand what resonates with your audience before paying to amplify it. Paid ads work best when you already have a clear message and a warm following.

**Can I appear in Brisbane vet waiting rooms?**
Yes, and it is very worthwhile. Ask the practice manager about their referral board or brochure display. A simple, warm A5 card with your credential, service description, and contact details is sufficient.
