## Google Business Profile

A **Google Business Profile** (GBP) is free and is essential for local search visibility. When someone types "pet bereavement support Edinburgh" or "pet loss counsellor near me" in Edinburgh, the map results that appear near the top of the page draw from GBP listings.

Set up your GBP at business.google.com. If you practise online, choose "online and visiting" as your delivery method and set your service area to Edinburgh and the Lothians rather than a physical address.

Write your business description using phrases that reflect real search behaviour: "pet bereavement support," "pet loss counsellor," "support after losing a dog," "help after a cat dies," "Edinburgh pet bereavement." Upload a professional photograph. Ask your first two or three clients to leave a review; even a small number of reviews places you above the majority of competitors in Edinburgh who currently have none.

Check and update your GBP every month, particularly your hours and availability.

## Social Media: Channels That Work in Edinburgh

Edinburgh's social media landscape has some specific characteristics worth understanding before you invest time.

**Facebook** remains the highest-reach platform for the core demographic of bereaved pet owners (broadly 30-65, though grief spans every age). Edinburgh has active local Facebook groups for neighbourhoods including Morningside, Bruntsfield, Stockbridge, Leith, and Marchmont. These groups are well-moderated and community-oriented; a thoughtful introduction post about your services, where group rules permit, will reach exactly the right local audience. The Edinburgh Dog Owners Facebook group is a particularly valuable community for your positioning.

**Instagram** builds long-term brand warmth and is well-used across the 30-55 age group. Post content that is genuinely useful or emotionally resonant: what pet grief looks like, the specific quality of a dog who waited at the door each evening, how to talk to children about pet death. Consistency matters more than production quality; a real, warm, imperfect post performs better in this space than a polished but distant one.

**LinkedIn** is the channel for professional referral development. Edinburgh's professional community, including veterinary managers, HR professionals, and wellbeing practitioners, is active on LinkedIn. Position yourself as a subject-matter expert: write about the professional case for acknowledging pet loss in the workplace, what TRACE certification means for client safety, and the evidence behind grief frameworks. This approach generates vet practice and corporate referrals over time and reinforces your credibility in Edinburgh's tightly connected professional network.

## Referral Marketing in Edinburgh: The Fastest Route to First Clients

Edinburgh's small, networked character means referral relationships generate results faster here than in almost any other UK city. One warm introduction from a well-connected vet or SSPCA coordinator can generate a string of clients.

**Veterinary practices.** Independent practices in Morningside, Bruntsfield, and Stockbridge are your highest-priority targets. These are neighbourhood clinics where the practice manager knows their clients personally and a referral to a trusted practitioner can happen in a single phone call. Write a professional one-page introduction, print it on quality paper or headed notepaper, and deliver it in person. Follow up by email one week later. The key message is simple: you are a TRACE-certified pet bereavement practitioner based in Edinburgh, you take self-referred clients, and you are happy to be added to their signposting sheet. Vets encounter bereaved clients every single week; you are offering them a solution to a real gap in their client care.

**Edinburgh Cremation Services for Pets.** This business meets families at the rawest point of their grief. Families who use them need ongoing support in the weeks and months ahead. A brief, respectful professional introduction and a supply of your business cards can generate steady warm referrals. Frame your outreach around the aftercare gap: you provide continuity of emotional support after their service ends.

**Scottish SPCA (SSPCA).** Edinburgh's primary animal welfare charity. Contact their community engagement team and introduce your services. People who lose animals through surrender, accident, or sudden illness are often left with unacknowledged grief.

**Edinburgh Dog and Cat Home.** Works with adopters, fosters, and surrendering owners. All of these can involve pet loss and subsequent grief. A relationship with their community engagement coordinator puts your name in front of a steady flow of people who may need what you offer.

**Dog trainers and groomers.** Often overlooked, these practitioners have long-term client relationships and hear about loss when it happens. A supply of business cards and a brief conversation can generate consistent referrals from people who would otherwise not know where to turn.

## Content Marketing: SEO and AI Search Together

A simple blog or resources section on your website, updated once or twice per month, builds search visibility for both Google and AI-powered answer engines including ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's AI Overview.

Write content that answers real questions:

- "How long does pet grief last?"
- "Is it normal to feel this sad when a pet dies?"
- "What is the TRACE method for pet loss support?"
- "How to find a pet bereavement counsellor in Edinburgh"
- "What happens in a pet loss support session"

Answer the question directly in the first paragraph. Use headings that mirror the question. Include Edinburgh naturally throughout. AI answer engines surface content that is clear, specific, and factually grounded; this type of writing simultaneously serves Google search and AI-generated answers.

One article per month is sufficient to begin building authority. Quality and specificity matter more than frequency.

## Paid Advertising

Paid advertising is not necessary in the first three months, but it can accelerate growth once your offer and website are tested.

**Google Ads.** Search ads appear at the top of results when someone in Edinburgh types "pet bereavement counsellor Edinburgh" or similar. Budget: start with £150-£250 per month. Use phrase and exact match keywords rather than broad match to avoid wasting budget. Your ad should lead to a dedicated landing page with one clear call-to-action (book a free 20-minute introductory call), not your home page.

**Facebook and Instagram Ads.** Meta's advertising platform allows you to target specific Edinburgh postcodes by age and interest (pets, dogs, cats). A boosted post targeting Edinburgh residents aged 30-65 with an interest in animals costs approximately £50-£100 for meaningful reach. Use a specific offer such as a free introductory call rather than a generic brand awareness message.

In Edinburgh, given the strength of word-of-mouth and referral networks, free channels will likely generate your first 10-15 clients. Add paid advertising once your positioning is tested and you have at least one or two positive reviews.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much should I spend on advertising when starting in Edinburgh?**
In the first three months, invest in free channels: your Academy listing, Google Business Profile, local Facebook group activity, and veterinary referral outreach. These will generate your first clients without cost. Add a modest Google Ads budget once your website and offer are tested.

**Is Facebook or Instagram more important for an Edinburgh practice?**
Facebook, because of its local neighbourhood group structure. Instagram builds long-term brand warmth but generates fewer direct enquiries in the short term.

**How long before content marketing generates traffic?**
Three to six months for Google search results to reflect new content. AI search tools can surface content more quickly. Start publishing now, since the value compounds over time.

**Do I need a website to advertise in Edinburgh?**
Not on day one. Your Academy listing and Google Business Profile cover you initially. A website within the first three months provides a destination for all other advertising and increases credibility significantly.

**Is print advertising worth considering in Edinburgh?**
Local Edinburgh print (neighbourhood newsletters in Bruntsfield, Morningside, Stockbridge) is inexpensive and reaches a hyper-local audience. Worth testing if you are building an in-person or hybrid practice, but not a first priority.
