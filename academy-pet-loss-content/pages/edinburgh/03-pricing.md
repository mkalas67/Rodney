# How to Price Your Pet Loss Support Sessions in Edinburgh

Edinburgh has one of the highest rates of private wellbeing service use outside London, a highly educated professional population, and almost no specialist pet bereavement practitioners. This combination means that if you price thoughtfully and present your work professionally, you can build an economically sustainable practice significantly faster than in most UK cities. This guide gives you the specific figures, session structures, and income scenarios you need to make confident pricing decisions from the start.

---

## What the Edinburgh Market Currently Looks Like

Edinburgh's private therapy and wellbeing sector is mature and relatively expensive by Scottish standards. Independent therapists and counsellors in the city typically charge £60-£90 per session, and established practitioners regularly exceed this. Clients in the Morningside, Bruntsfield, Stockbridge, and New Town areas are accustomed to professional fees in this range and do not baulk at them if the practitioner presents credibly.

Edinburgh also has a smaller, more word-of-mouth-driven market than Birmingham or Manchester. This cuts both ways: you are more visible once established, but early positioning matters more. Practitioners who set their prices too low from the start find it harder to raise them without disrupting relationships built on that original rate.

The TRACE Practitioner Certificate positions your work explicitly within a recognised evidence-based framework, which is the professional justification for charging Edinburgh market rates rather than the lower rates that might apply to an uncertified practitioner.

Current market benchmarks for Edinburgh pet bereavement support:

**Early-career practitioners (newly certified, building a caseload):**
- Single session: £55-£70
- Three-session package: £145-£185
- Six-session package: £270-£350

**Established practitioners (12 months or more, active referral base):**
- Single session: £80-£115
- Three-session package: £215-£305
- Six-session package: £400-£575

These figures are based on 50-60 minute sessions. For extended 75-90 minute sessions, add £15-£25 to each band. Edinburgh rates sit at the higher end of Scotland and the upper-mid range for the UK overall.

## Session Structures That Work

**Single session.** Best for clients in acute grief who need support now and are not ready to commit to a programme, or for clients who want to test the experience before a longer engagement. Single sessions are also appropriate for memorial occasions: the anniversary of a pet's death, a birthday, or the date of euthanasia. Price at your standard rate.

**Three-session package.** The most commonly booked structure. It provides enough space to move through the full TRACE framework: Tell the Story and Recognise the Bond typically occupy the first session; Acknowledge the Pain, Celebrate the Life, and Embrace What Remains unfold over sessions two and three. Request full or part payment in advance. Offer a modest discount of around 10% versus the single session rate as an incentive for commitment.

**Six-session package.** Appropriate for complex grief, multiple losses, or clients who prefer a sustained and clearly bounded container. A 15% discount from single-session rate is a reasonable incentive without devaluing your work.

**Free introductory call (20 minutes).** Offer this as a standard first contact. It is not a free session; it is a brief, structured conversation in which the client explains their situation and you explain your approach. It removes the financial risk of a first contact and substantially increases conversion from enquiry to booked session. The majority of practitioners who use this structure report that most introductory calls convert to paid bookings.

Have a cancellation policy in place from day one and state it clearly at booking and in your confirmation email. A 24-hour notice requirement is standard and protects your income without being onerous.

You set your own price for these. The standard market rate is approximately $99 per page (around £80 at current exchange rates). Edinburgh practitioners typically price these at £75-£95 per page.

If you work with 20 new clients in your first year and 50% take up a memorial page, your first 10 pages are worth approximately £750-£950. After your first 10 pages, additional pages are available through the Academy at standard rates with your year-one 50% loyalty discount applied.

Edinburgh clients, particularly those who have lost older dogs or cats with whom they had a long, close relationship, respond very well to the memorial page offer. The city has a strong culture of commemoration and remembrance; presenting the memorial page as a lasting tribute to a specific, irreplaceable animal tends to land warmly. Frame it as an offering, not an upsell.

## When to Raise Your Prices

Raise your rates when:

- You consistently have a short waiting list rather than immediate availability.
- You have been practising for 12 months or more.
- You have positive reviews or testimonials that you can point to.
- Enquiries are coming through your Academy listing or Google Business Profile regularly.

Do not stay at an early-career rate beyond the first year out of habit or imposter syndrome. Raising rates by £10-£15 per session after 12 months is normal professional progression and is unlikely to affect client retention.

Give existing clients at least four weeks' notice of any price change and honour their current rate for any pre-booked sessions.

## Sliding Scale Considerations

Sliding scale pricing is optional but worth considering thoughtfully. Edinburgh has significant economic inequality beneath its prosperous surface. Areas including Leith, Wester Hailes, and Sighthill have substantially lower average incomes than Morningside or the New Town.

If you choose to offer a small number of reduced-rate spots, set a firm floor (a minimum you will not go below, such as £40) and limit the number of sliding scale spaces to two or three in your caseload. State that these are available, set out the basis (genuine financial barrier, not a general discount), and let clients self-identify.

Avoid making assumptions about who needs reduced fees based on where someone lives or their apparent background. Edinburgh's diversity of circumstance is less visible than its professional surface suggests.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should online and in-person sessions be priced the same in Edinburgh?**
Yes. Your time and expertise are equal regardless of medium. If you offer in-person sessions in a hired room (which incurs rental costs), you may add a small supplement of £5-£10 per session to cover room hire.

**How do I handle a client who asks for a reduction?**
Acknowledge their request warmly and, if you have a sliding scale space available, offer one. If not: "My current rate is £X. I do keep a small number of reduced-rate spaces for those with genuine financial barriers; at the moment they are not available, but I can let you know when one opens." You do not need to explain or justify your rate.

**Is it worth offering packages from the start?**
Yes. Packages improve cash flow and commit the client to the work. They also tend to produce better outcomes because the therapeutic arc has a clear shape.

**Are Edinburgh session rates genuinely higher than in other Scottish cities?**
Yes. Edinburgh consistently commands higher rates than Glasgow, Dundee, or Aberdeen for private wellbeing services. This reflects average incomes, professional culture, and the density of experienced practitioners who have established the ceiling of the market.

**When should I review my rates annually?**
Set a specific date each year (your practice anniversary is a natural choice) and review your rates against the Edinburgh market, your current caseload, and your waiting list position. Adjust if warranted; do not let rates stagnate over multiple years simply through inertia.
