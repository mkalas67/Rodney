Greater Sydney has a population of over five million, with some of the highest household pet ownership rates in the world. The demand for dedicated, skilled pet bereavement support exists throughout Greater Sydney, from the Inner West to the Northern Beaches to the Hills District, and it is almost entirely unmet.

At the same time, Australians have a practical relationship with professional services: they want to know what you do, what it costs, and what they will get. That directness works in your favour once your practice is clearly described and fairly priced.

## Business Structure in New South Wales

Setting up as a legitimate business in Australia is straightforward and inexpensive.

**ABN (Australian Business Number).** You need an ABN to operate as a business in Australia. Registration is free and completed online at [abr.gov.au](https://www.abr.gov.au). Most sole traders and small businesses register within a day. Your ABN appears on your invoices and is expected by any professional or business client.

**Business name registration.** If you trade under your own name (for example, Sarah Jones Pet Bereavement Support), you do not need to register a business name. If you use a trading name such as "Sydney Pet Loss Support" or "Gentle Endings Counselling," you must register it with ASIC (Australian Securities and Investments Commission). Business name registration costs approximately AUD $40 per year for a one-year registration or AUD $94 for three years. Register at [connectonline.asic.gov.au](https://connectonline.asic.gov.au).

**Incorporation.** If you want to incorporate as a company, this is also done through ASIC and costs AUD $597 for a proprietary company. Most solo practitioners start as sole traders and only consider incorporation later, if at all.

**GST Registration.** If your annual turnover exceeds AUD $75,000, you must register for GST. Below this threshold, registration is optional. Note that counselling and therapeutic support services are generally GST-free under the GST Act, but confirm the treatment of your specific service with an accountant.

**Professional Liability Insurance.** Public liability and professional indemnity insurance is strongly recommended. Several Australian insurers and broker platforms offer coverage for wellness and coaching practitioners at modest annual premiums.

## What Credentials Do You Need?

The title "counsellor" is largely unregulated in Australia. Unlike psychologists and social workers, counsellors do not need to be registered with AHPRA (the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency). Pet bereavement support as delivered through the TRACE framework is not a health service, so AHPRA registration does not apply.

No Australian law requires you to hold a specific qualification to offer compassionate pet grief support. What matters, from both a professional and marketing perspective, is that you hold a recognised certification that demonstrates structured training.

If you already hold a qualification in counselling, social work, nursing, veterinary practice, psychology, or a related field, that is a valuable addition to your professional profile. If you do not, the TRACE certification is designed as your primary qualification in this specialised area, and it is entirely sufficient for the kind of support you will be providing.

## Professional Presence

Before you see your first client, set up your professional presence.

**A simple website.** You need a homepage that explains what you do, who you help, how sessions work, and how to get in touch. A clear, warm professional photo and a brief bio are essential. Sydney clients will Google you before they call.

**Your Academy directory listing.** This is included with your certification and is the most targeted place a grieving pet owner in Sydney can find you. Complete it fully: location, services, session format, a warm and specific description of your approach, and your booking or contact link.

**Google Business Profile.** Set up a free profile at [business.google.com](https://business.google.com). This places you on Google Maps and in local search results when someone searches "pet bereavement counsellor Sydney" or "pet grief support near me." Set your service area to cover Greater Sydney and specify key regions such as Inner Sydney, North Shore, Northern Beaches, Inner West, Eastern Suburbs, and Western Sydney.

**Professional email.** Use a domain-based address (yourname@yourpracticename.com.au) rather than a free Gmail or Hotmail account. It costs very little and signals that you are running a professional practice.

## Getting Your First Clients in Sydney

**Veterinary clinics.** Vet practices are your highest-value referral partners. Sydney has hundreds of general practices and several specialist hospitals. Introduce yourself in writing and follow up in person where possible. Leave printed materials that reception staff can hand to clients after a loss. Focus initially on practices in your local area, then expand outward.

**SASH (Small Animal Specialist Hospital, North Ryde).** SASH is one of Australia's leading specialist animal hospitals and sees high volumes of complex and critical cases, many of which result in euthanasia or unexpected death. A professional introduction to their team can be a productive relationship.

**RSPCA NSW.** The RSPCA NSW has significant community presence across Greater Sydney and serves people who lose animals in their care. Contact their volunteer or community engagement team to introduce yourself as a certified pet bereavement practitioner.

**AWL NSW (Animal Welfare League).** Another major rescue and welfare organisation in NSW with community connections that translate into potential referrals.

**Sydney Dogs and Cats Home.** Based in St Peters, with connections to the wider Inner Sydney and Inner West community.

**PetBarn.** Australia's largest pet retail chain has strong community networks and in some locations runs community noticeboards or social media groups. A presence here, even informally, can generate awareness.

**Gateway Pet Memorial and Rainbow Bridge Pet Cremation.** Pet cremation and memorial businesses in Sydney encounter grieving clients at the most acute moment. A mutual referral arrangement with these businesses, a mention in their materials in exchange for your referrals, is natural and cost-free.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to be registered with any Australian authority to offer pet bereavement support?**
No. Pet bereavement counselling as delivered through the TRACE framework is not a regulated health service in Australia. AHPRA registration is not required.

**Can I work from home?**
Yes. Most pet bereavement sessions are delivered online or by telephone. You do not need a separate office space. If you meet clients in person, ensure the space is private and quiet.

**How quickly can I start seeing clients after completing the TRACE certification?**
As soon as your certification is complete and your professional presence is set up, you can begin. Most practitioners take two to four weeks to complete the Core Programme, though the self-paced format allows flexibility.

**Is this work eligible for Medicare or NDIS funding?**
No. Pet bereavement support is not covered under Medicare or the NDIS. Clients pay out of pocket. This is the norm in Australia for wellness and coaching services, and most clients understand and accept it.

**How much can I earn?**
At AUD $100 to $170 per session starting out, and AUD $145 to $220 as an established practitioner, a part-time practice of ten sessions per week generates AUD $52,000 to $95,000 per year. Add memorial page revenue and the income potential is substantial.
