# How to Price Your Pet Loss Counselling Sessions in Sydney

Pricing is one of the decisions that new practitioners overthink and underdo. The temptation is to charge very little at the start, reasoning that low prices attract clients quickly. In practice, undercharging signals a lack of confidence, attracts clients who undervalue the work, and makes your practice financially unsustainable before it has a chance to grow.

Sydney is a large and affluent market. People here pay for naturopathy, life coaching, wellness retreats, and specialist consultations without hesitation. A well-positioned, certified pet bereavement practitioner sits comfortably within that landscape and can charge professional rates from the beginning.

This page covers current market benchmarks in Australian dollars, session structure options, the additional revenue stream from memorial pages, realistic income projections, and how to approach rate increases confidently.

## What Pet Loss Counsellors Charge in Sydney

There is no formal pricing body for pet bereavement support in Australia. That gives you flexibility, but it also means you need reference points from the broader wellness and counselling market.

**Early-career practitioners (first twelve to eighteen months):**
AUD $100 to $170 per session. This range positions you firmly in the professional wellness tier from the outset. It is accessible to most Sydney clients who seek this type of support while still reflecting the specialised nature of the work.

**Established practitioners (eighteen months and beyond, with referrals and visible online presence):**
AUD $145 to $220 per session. This is consistent with what experienced wellness and coaching practitioners charge in Sydney and reflects the credibility of a practitioner with testimonials, a full Google profile, and a referral network.

For context, general counsellors and psychotherapists in Sydney charge AUD $120 to $280 per session. Life coaches typically charge AUD $120 to $250. Pet bereavement support, because it is highly specialised and the need is acute, sits solidly within the mid-to-upper part of this range.

At current exchange rates, this is approximately AUD $610 to $810 as a one-time investment, recoverable within five to eight sessions.

**Medicare and NDIS.** Pet bereavement support is not covered under Medicare or the NDIS. Clients pay out of pocket. This is the standard for wellness and coaching services in Australia and does not materially affect your ability to build a full practice. Clients who seek out dedicated pet bereavement support have already decided they want and need it.

## Session Structures

How you package and present sessions shapes both the client experience and your income stability.

**Single session.** A standalone ninety-minute session works well for clients who have already moved through the first acute phase and want one structured conversation to process what has happened. Price at or slightly above your standard hourly rate.

**Three-session package.** Three sessions over four to six weeks allows you to move through the full TRACE framework at a natural pace. A package price of AUD $285 to $490 (depending on your rate stage) gives the client a modest saving, commits them to the process, and improves your cash flow predictability. Most clients find that three sessions provide meaningful resolution, which makes this the most natural starting package.

**Single follow-up session.** Offer a standalone follow-up session at your standard rate for clients who completed a package and want to check in around an anniversary, a difficult season, or a decision about a new pet. These are easy to book and high in perceived value.

**Decision-support intensive.** Some clients need help with a specific, time-pressured decision, most commonly whether to proceed with euthanasia. A longer single session (two to three hours, with breaks) designed around this specific need can be priced at two to two-and-a-half times your standard hourly rate. This format is uncommon in the Sydney market and can differentiate your practice.

## Memorial Pages as a Second Revenue Stream

The TRACE certification includes the ability to create and host memorial pages for clients through the Academy for Pet Loss platform. These are dedicated, permanent pages celebrating a specific animal's life, with photographs, stories, and a lasting online record.

You set the price for memorial pages yourself. The industry benchmark is USD $99 per page, hosted for three years. In the Sydney market, this translates to approximately AUD $150 to $165 per page, which is a reasonable and easily justified price for a lasting, personalised memorial.

Memorial pages can be offered as a standalone service or bundled into a session package. A "complete memorial package," combining three TRACE sessions with a memorial page, is a natural premium product with a total price in the range of AUD $430 to $645 depending on your rate stage. This covers the emotional need end-to-end and commands a premium accordingly.

## Realistic Income Projections for a Sydney Practice

A part-time practice with ten sessions per week at an average of AUD $130 produces AUD $1,300 per week, or approximately AUD $62,400 per year before expenses. This is achievable within twelve to eighteen months for a practitioner who actively markets themselves and maintains their professional presence.

A full practice (twenty or more sessions per week) at established rates of AUD $165 to $190 per session produces AUD $3,300 to $3,800 per week, or approximately AUD $160,000 to $185,000 annually. This requires a strong referral network, consistent content marketing, and a fully optimised directory and Google presence, but it is a realistic ceiling for a dedicated and well-positioned practitioner in a city the size of Sydney.

Add revenue from memorial pages, occasional intensive sessions, and potential workshop or group offerings over time, and the income potential is genuinely substantial.

## When to Raise Your Rates

Raise your rates when:

- You are consistently turning away clients or operating with a waitlist.
- You have at least five strong client testimonials or Google reviews.
- You have been practising for twelve to eighteen months and feel confident in your method.
- Your referral network is generating steady enquiries without paid advertising.

When you raise rates, give existing clients four to six weeks notice and offer them the option to book additional sessions at the current rate before the increase takes effect. A brief, professional message is all that is needed. There is no need to apologise or over-explain.

Rate increases every twelve to eighteen months are normal and expected in the Australian wellness market.

## Sliding Scale Pricing

Whether to offer sliding scale or reduced-fee sessions is a personal decision. Some practitioners reserve two or three spots per month for clients in genuine financial hardship, particularly those referred through rescue and welfare organisations such as RSPCA NSW or AWL NSW.

If you offer a sliding scale, set a minimum that still feels respectful of your time. In Sydney, a minimum of AUD $60 to $80 per session is appropriate. State clearly on your website how many reduced-fee spots are available and how to enquire. Do not negotiate reduced rates under pressure during a booking conversation. If you want to help someone, offer a reduced-fee spot explicitly, on your terms.

Offering too many discounted sessions is unsustainable and creates resentment. Protect your capacity.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I charge the same for online and in-person sessions?**
Yes. The value of the session is in your expertise and the TRACE framework, not the location. Charging less for online sessions devalues the work and creates a precedent that is difficult to reverse.

**How do I handle payment in Australia?**
Bank transfer (direct bank deposit) is widely used for personal services in Australia. Provide your BSB and account number in your booking confirmation. Stripe is well-suited for website checkout and card payments and integrates cleanly with scheduling tools. PayPal is familiar to many clients as an alternative.

Collect payment before or at the start of the session. A clear cancellation policy (twenty-four hours notice for a refund or rebooking credit) protects your time.

**Do I need to charge GST?**
If your annual revenue is below AUD $75,000, you are not required to register for GST. Above this threshold, registration is mandatory. Counselling and therapeutic support services are generally treated as GST-free under the GST Act, but the exact treatment of your service type should be confirmed with an Australian accountant.

**What if a client cannot afford my rates?**
You are entitled to say that your rates are set and not negotiable. If you want to help someone in genuine hardship, offer a reduced-fee spot on your own terms rather than negotiating under pressure.
