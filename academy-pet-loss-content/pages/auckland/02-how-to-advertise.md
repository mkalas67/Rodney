# How to Advertise Your Pet Loss Counselling Practice in Auckland

Advertising a pet bereavement counselling practice in Auckland does not require a large budget or a background in marketing. What it does require is clarity about who you are, what you offer, and where the people who need you are already looking. This guide covers every meaningful channel available to you, with specific guidance on what works in the Auckland context.

---

## Start With Your Academy for Pet Loss Directory Listing

Before you spend time or money on any other channel, make sure your Academy for Pet Loss directory listing is complete and optimised. Your TRACE certification includes a one-year premium listing in the Academy's directory, and this listing has real search value. People searching for "pet loss support New Zealand" or "pet bereavement practitioner Auckland" may land on the Academy directory before they find individual practitioner websites.

- Auckland as your location, plus confirmation that you offer online sessions (this matters because clients may be anywhere in New Zealand)

- Your session types, pricing range, and how to contact you or book

A complete, professional directory listing is one of the highest-return actions you can take in the first week after certification.

## Google Business Profile

A Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is free and places you in Google Maps results and in the "local pack" that appears at the top of search results when someone searches for services near them.

To set one up, go to business.google.com and create a profile. Choose a category such as "Counsellor," "Mental Health Service," or "Life Coach," as there is no specific pet bereavement category. In your business description, use plain language that reflects how clients search: "pet loss support," "bereavement support after losing a pet," "help after a pet dies."

If you work from home, you can set your profile to show a service area (for example, Greater Auckland, or all of New Zealand) rather than a home address. This is a common and appropriate setting for practitioners who work online or by appointment at neutral venues.

Gather reviews actively. After a positive engagement with a client or a referrer, ask directly. Google reviews are a significant trust signal and they compound over time.

Respond to every review, positive or otherwise. A thoughtful, professional response to a review signals to prospective clients how you communicate.

## Social Media: Where Auckland Pet Owners Are

**Facebook**
Facebook is the dominant social media platform for the Auckland audience most likely to need pet bereavement support. This is particularly true for pet owners aged 30 and above. A Facebook Page for your practice is free and allows you to post content, receive messages, and be found in Facebook search.

Content that works on Facebook for this audience includes:

- Short personal reflections on the nature of pet grief (written warmly and in your own voice)
- Information that normalises pet loss grief ("It is okay to grieve deeply for an animal")
- Details about what a session looks like and how the TRACE framework works
- Relevant community events such as pet memorial days or awareness content

Facebook Groups are a strong complementary tool. Auckland has active pet owner groups, dog owner groups, and local community groups. You should not directly advertise in most of these groups, but contributing thoughtfully to conversations about pet health, loss, and grief builds your presence and reputation over time.

**Instagram**
Instagram skews younger and more visual than Facebook. It can be effective for reaching the 25 to 40 Auckland demographic, particularly the urban pet owner segment. Focus on warm, unhurried imagery: candles, nature, animals at peace. Captions should be reflective and personal. You do not need to post frequently. Three to four times per week is enough to maintain a meaningful presence.

**LinkedIn**
LinkedIn is increasingly relevant for building referral relationships rather than reaching clients directly. Auckland's professional networks are active on LinkedIn, and vets, animal welfare workers, social workers, and funeral directors are all present. Posting thoughtful content about pet grief, the TRACE approach, and the professional gap in this space positions you as a credible specialist in conversations with potential referrers.

## Referral Marketing: The Highest-Value Channel in Auckland

New Zealand is a small country, and Auckland is a city where professional and community networks overlap significantly. A single strong referral relationship can sustain a meaningful portion of your practice.

**Vet Practices**
Veterinary practices are your single most important referral source. Every vet in Auckland encounters grieving pet owners, and most feel under-equipped to help beyond the clinical moment. Practices in Epsom, Parnell, Henderson, Albany, and Manukau are all worth approaching. A short, professional introductory letter or visit, followed by a simple one-page information sheet about what you offer, is enough to begin.

The key message to vets is: "I take this client off your hands at the hardest moment. I am trained, structured, and I will look after them."

**Pet Cremation Services**
Pet Cremation NZ and Peaceful Pets NZ see grieving families at the most acute point of loss. A referral arrangement or a simple information card left at their counter gives you access to clients at exactly the right time. Reach out professionally and explain the gap you fill.

**SPCA Auckland and Auckland Council Animal Management**
The SPCA Auckland branch and Auckland Council Animal Management both encounter animal loss scenarios regularly. These organisations are generally supportive of service providers who fill real community needs. Introduce yourself and provide information about your practice.

**Dog Trainers and Groomers**
These professionals often have long-term relationships with pet owners. They hear about illness, ageing, and loss before the vet does in many cases. A referral from a trusted groomer or trainer carries significant weight.

**Rural and Farming Community Connections**
The wider Auckland region extends into rural areas where working animals, particularly farm dogs and horses, are deeply significant. Farmers rarely seek emotional support but often need it. This is an underserved niche. If you have personal connections to rural communities, this is worth developing.

## Content Marketing

A modest but consistent content marketing effort builds your search visibility and establishes your expertise over time.

**Blog or Articles on Your Website**
Write short, genuinely useful articles about topics your prospective clients search for. Examples include: "Is it normal to grieve this much for a pet?", "What to say to someone who has lost a pet," "How to talk to your children about a pet dying." Target long-tail search terms that reflect real questions.

**Guest Content**
Offer to write a short piece for a local vet practice newsletter, an SPCA publication, or a pet owner community group. This builds your credibility and your backlink profile simultaneously.

## Paid Advertising

Paid advertising is not necessary in the early stages of your practice, but it can accelerate visibility when you are ready to invest.

**Google Ads**
A modest Google Ads campaign targeting terms such as "pet loss support Auckland," "pet bereavement counselling NZ," and "help after losing a pet New Zealand" can generate direct enquiries. A starting budget of NZD $15 to $25 per day is enough to test performance. Search ads are more effective than display ads for this type of service because people are actively looking.

**Facebook Ads**
Facebook's targeting allows you to reach Auckland pet owners specifically. A boosted post or a simple awareness ad with a warm, non-salesy message can build your page following and generate enquiries. A test budget of NZD $100 to $200 is reasonable for an initial run of two to three weeks.

**When to Start Paid Ads**
Wait until you have a complete website, an active Google Business Profile, and at least one or two client testimonials before investing in paid advertising. Paid traffic sent to an incomplete or unconvincing web presence is wasted.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the most cost-effective advertising for a new Auckland practice?**
A complete Academy directory listing and Google Business Profile are both free and often more effective than paid advertising in the early stages. Word-of-mouth referrals from vets and pet cremation services are the fastest path to early clients.

**Do I need a website before I start advertising?**
Yes. Every other channel, including your Google Business Profile, social media, and directory listing, should point somewhere. A simple, clear website is the foundation.

**Should I be on all social media platforms?**
No. Start with Facebook, which has the best reach for your Auckland audience. Add Instagram if you enjoy visual content. Consider LinkedIn for professional networking. You do not need to be on TikTok, Twitter/X, or Pinterest to build a viable local practice.

**How long before I see results from referral outreach?**
Most practitioners see their first client within four to eight weeks of beginning structured referral outreach to vets and cremation services. Persistence matters. A vet may not have a suitable client for your service for several weeks after first contact.
