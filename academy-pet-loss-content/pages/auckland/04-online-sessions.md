# How to Run Online Pet Loss Counselling Sessions in Auckland

Online delivery is not a compromise for a pet bereavement support practice. For many clients, it is the preferred format. This guide explains why online sessions work particularly well for this kind of support, walks you through the practical setup, and covers the specific platform, scheduling, payment, and privacy considerations relevant to Auckland-based practitioners in New Zealand.

---

## Why Online Sessions Suit Pet Loss Support

Pet bereavement support has characteristics that make online delivery genuinely effective, sometimes more effective than in-person work.

**Grief is best processed in comfort.** Many clients find it easier to open up about the loss of a beloved animal when they are in their own home, surrounded by familiar objects, and without the anxiety of travelling to an unfamiliar location. A client may want to have their pet's ashes nearby, or to show you a photograph on the wall. This is natural and possible in an online format.

**The conversation is the whole service.** Unlike some health or therapeutic modalities, pet bereavement support as delivered through the TRACE framework is almost entirely verbal and relational. Approximately 97% of the work is compassionate presence and structured conversation. There is no physical examination or hands-on component. Online delivery loses almost nothing compared to in-person.

**Geography is not a barrier.** New Zealand is a long, narrow country with a relatively dispersed population. Auckland is the largest city, but there are significant populations in Hamilton, Tauranga, Wellington, Christchurch, Dunedin, and across rural regions who have no local access to pet bereavement support. Offering online sessions means you can serve any New Zealander with an internet connection.

**Clients may be in acute distress.** Someone who has just lost a pet may not be in a state to drive to an appointment. Online sessions allow you to be available when a client needs you most, without requiring them to manage travel and logistics on top of grief.

## Platform Comparison: What to Use

**Zoom (Recommended)**
Zoom is the most widely used video platform in New Zealand for professional consultations and telehealth, and it is the recommended choice for pet bereavement support sessions. Your clients are very likely already familiar with Zoom. It is stable, easy to use on desktop and mobile, and offers a professional impression.

The free Zoom plan allows 40-minute meetings with a single participant, which is limiting for a 60-minute session. The Pro plan costs approximately USD $15 per month (or NZD $25 to $30 per month billed in NZD) and removes this restriction. For a professional practice, the Pro plan is worth the cost.

Zoom's waiting room feature is useful. Clients enter a waiting room and you admit them when you are ready, which gives you control over transitions between sessions.

**Microsoft Teams**
Teams is widely used in corporate and public sector contexts in New Zealand, but it is less familiar to general consumers and slightly more complex to set up for clients who do not already have a Microsoft account. It is a reasonable backup option but not the recommended first choice.

**FaceTime**
FaceTime is simple and works well for clients who have Apple devices, but it limits your client base to Apple users and does not allow for professional features such as waiting rooms, recording (if you ever want that capability), or scheduling links. It is appropriate for informal follow-up calls but not for primary sessions.

**Google Meet**
Google Meet is free, reliable, and accessible via a browser link without requiring an app. It is a usable alternative if a client has difficulty with Zoom, but it lacks the professional features of the Zoom Pro plan.

**Phone Calls**
Do not overlook the phone. Some clients, particularly older clients or those in rural areas with limited broadband, will be more comfortable on a voice call. The quality of care you deliver through the TRACE framework is not diminished by the absence of video. Offer phone as an option alongside video.

## Tech Setup for Auckland Practitioners

Your technical setup does not need to be expensive. It needs to be reliable and professional.

**Internet Connection**
A stable broadband connection is essential. Most Auckland residential broadband is sufficient. If you work in a space with unreliable wifi, use a wired ethernet connection for sessions. Mobile data (4G/5G) is a usable backup.

**Camera**
The built-in camera on a modern laptop is adequate for most sessions. If you want a more professional image, an external webcam (Logitech C920 or similar, available at Noel Leeming or PB Tech in Auckland for around NZD $100 to $160) makes a noticeable difference.

**Microphone and Audio**
Audio quality matters more than video quality in an emotional support context. A headset with a built-in microphone removes background noise and keeps your voice consistent. Many Bluetooth headsets work well. Alternatively, a simple USB desktop microphone (NZD $60 to $120) is effective. Do not rely solely on a laptop's built-in microphone if you can avoid it.

**Lighting**
Sit facing a window or use a small ring light (NZD $30 to $80 from Kmart or online). Good lighting makes the session feel warmer and more human. Avoid sitting with a bright window behind you, as this creates a silhouette effect.

**Background**
Keep your background tidy and neutral. A bookshelf, a plain wall, or a simple indoor plant communicates professionalism. If your home environment is difficult to manage, use Zoom's virtual background feature with a plain, calm image.

**Quiet Space**
Choose a room where you will not be interrupted during sessions. A door that closes and a brief message to others in your household before each session is sufficient. Clients in grief need to feel they have your full, uninterrupted attention.

## Client Experience for Online Sessions

The client's experience of an online session depends heavily on how you set it up.

**Before the Session**
Send a short confirmation email or message 24 hours before the session. Include the Zoom link, the scheduled time in New Zealand Standard Time (or NZDT during daylight saving), a note about what to have ready (a comfortable seat, a glass of water, perhaps a photo of their pet if they want to share it), and your cancellation policy.

**At the Start of the Session**
Take two to three minutes at the opening to acknowledge any tech issues, check that the client is comfortable, and establish that they are in a private space where they can speak freely. This is especially important for online sessions where you cannot assess the physical environment.

**During the Session**
Use the TRACE framework as your guide. The online format does not change the framework. Tell the Story, Recognise the Bond, Acknowledge the Pain, Celebrate the Life, Embrace What Remains. The conversation flows the same way whether you are in the same room or connecting across New Zealand.

**Closing the Session**
Allow a few minutes at the end to bring the session to a clear close. Summarise what was covered, confirm any next steps, and let the client know how to reach you if they need anything before the next session. Abrupt endings are jarring online. A warm, unhurried close matters.

## Scheduling and Payment for Auckland Online Clients

**Scheduling**
Acuity Scheduling and Calendly are both widely used by New Zealand service providers for online booking. Both integrate with Zoom to generate automatic meeting links. Both allow you to set your available hours in New Zealand time. Acuity's paid plan (around USD $16 per month) includes intake forms that you can use to gather basic information before the first session.

**Payment**
In New Zealand, bank transfer (direct debit from a NZ bank account) is the dominant payment method for professional services and is entirely appropriate for session fees. PayPal is widely used and familiar to NZ clients. Stripe allows you to take card payments via an invoice or payment link and is fully supported in New Zealand.

Paymark and EFTPOS are card payment systems designed for in-person retail transactions. They are not suitable for remote payment collection. Do not attempt to use them for online session payments.

Request payment before the session (or immediately after the first session if the client is new). A simple payment link via Stripe or a bank transfer reference in your confirmation email is sufficient.

## Privacy and Your Legal Obligations in New Zealand

Pet bereavement support is not a health service regulated under the Health and Disability Commissioner Act or the Health Practitioners Competence Assurance Act. You are not a clinical practitioner, and your clients are not patients. You do not have the same documentation and privacy obligations as a registered health provider.

However, the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 applies to any personal information you collect and hold. This means:

- Collect only the information you need (name, contact details, brief background relevant to the sessions).
- Store it securely. Notes kept digitally should be password-protected. Email correspondence with clients should be treated as confidential.
- Do not share client information with third parties without consent.
- If a client asks what information you hold about them, you must be able to respond and provide it if requested.

You do not need to produce a lengthy privacy policy, but a short, plain-language statement on your website and/or in your client agreement covering how you handle information is appropriate and professional.

Session recording should only happen with explicit prior written consent from the client. The safest practice is not to record sessions unless there is a specific agreed reason.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I run sessions for clients outside Auckland if I am based in Auckland?**
Yes. There are no geographic restrictions on providing online support services within New Zealand. You can work with clients in any part of the country.

**Can I work with clients in Australia or other countries?**
Yes, with some caveats. Check whether the client's country has any specific rules around online counselling or support services. For Australian clients, the Australian Consumer Law may apply to your services. For most pet loss support work, this is a straightforward matter of being clear about what you offer and ensuring the client understands it.

**What if a client becomes very distressed during an online session?**
Have a brief safety plan in mind. If a client expresses distress that goes beyond what the TRACE framework is designed to support (for example, acute mental health crisis or thoughts of self-harm), your role is to compassionately acknowledge this and refer them to appropriate professional support. In New Zealand, Lifeline (0800 543 354) and 1737 (free text and call) are key crisis support lines. Have these details ready.

**Is Zoom safe for confidential client conversations?**
Zoom's paid plans offer end-to-end encryption and other security features appropriate for confidential professional conversations. Enable the waiting room feature and use a unique meeting link for each client rather than a fixed personal meeting room link.
