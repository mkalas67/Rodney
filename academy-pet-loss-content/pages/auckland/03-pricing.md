# How to Price Your Pet Loss Counselling Sessions in Auckland

Pricing is one of the decisions that new practitioners get most stuck on. Charge too little and you signal that the service lacks value, or you burn out without a sustainable income. Charge too much before you have established yourself and you create a barrier to your first clients. This guide gives you the specific numbers relevant to Auckland, explains how to structure your sessions and packages, and helps you build toward income that makes the practice genuinely worthwhile.

---

## What Pet Loss Counselling Sessions Cost in Auckland

There is no official regulated rate for pet bereavement support in New Zealand, because the field is not regulated. Rates are set by individual practitioners based on their experience, credentials, location, and the market's willingness to pay.

Based on what practitioners in Auckland and New Zealand more broadly are charging for comparable non-clinical support services, here is a realistic framework:

**Early-career practitioners (first one to two years, building a client base):**
NZD $100 to $170 per session

**Established practitioners (two or more years, strong referral base, recognised credentials):**
NZD $140 to $210 per session

These rates reflect a standard 50 to 60 minute session. They are broadly consistent with what New Zealanders pay for life coaching, wellbeing coaching, and other non-clinical support services.

**A note on currency:** The TRACE programme itself is priced in USD at $395 for the Core Programme and $525 for the Extended Programme. Your Auckland practice will operate in NZD.

If you are aware of the broader NZ counselling landscape, registered counsellors and psychologists charge NZD $150 to $250 per session for clinical work. Your rates as a pet loss support practitioner sit below that range, which is appropriate given the non-clinical nature of the work and the lower barrier to entry.

## Session Structures That Work

**Single Session**
Useful for clients who want a one-off conversation, perhaps shortly after a pet dies, or to assess whether ongoing support feels right. Priced at your standard per-session rate. Single sessions are easy to sell, low-commitment for the client, and often convert to packages once the client experiences the value.

**Three-Session Package**
A practical starter package. Three sessions allow you to work through the key elements of the TRACE framework in a structured way. Priced at two and a half times your single session rate (a modest discount that rewards commitment). For example, if your session rate is NZD $130, a three-session package might be NZD $320.

**Five-Session Package**
A deeper engagement that allows real work across all five steps of TRACE plus space for integration and closure. Priced at four to four and a half times your single session rate. This is often where the most meaningful and lasting work happens.

**Memorial Consultation**
A distinct offering focused on creating a memorial page for a client's pet. This is a separate service from ongoing support sessions and is typically priced differently. See the Memorial Pages section below.

**Phone Check-In**
A short 20 to 30 minute follow-up call between sessions or after a programme completes. Not suitable as a primary service, but useful as a complimentary touchpoint that builds client loyalty and word-of-mouth referrals.

## Memorial Pages: A Separate Revenue Stream

Your TRACE Core Programme certification entitles you to create ten memorial pages on the Academy for Pet Loss platform, with each page hosted for three years. The standard pricing for memorial pages is USD $99 per page.

In Auckland, you may choose to price memorial pages in NZD. At current exchange rates, USD $99 is approximately NZD $165 to $175. Pricing in NZD $150 to $170 is a reasonable range that reflects local purchasing norms.

Memorial pages are a meaningful offering. Many clients want a permanent, beautiful record of their pet. You create the page with the client, using content and photos they provide, as a structured act of remembrance. It is often the most tangible part of the TRACE work and clients deeply value it.

You can offer memorial pages as a standalone service or as part of a package. For example, a five-session package could include one memorial page as part of the offering.

## When to Raise Your Rates

You should consider raising your rates when:

- You are consistently at 80% or more of your available session slots
- You have been practising for at least 12 months and have completed the TRACE programme
- You have received consistent positive feedback and testimonials
- You have established referral relationships that provide a steady flow of enquiries

Raise rates by incremental steps of NZD $10 to $20 per session rather than large jumps. Existing long-term clients can be held at their current rate or given a shorter transitional period before the new rate applies.

## Sliding Scale and Access Pricing

Pet loss does not respect income brackets. Some of the people who most need support are those on fixed incomes, those in rural areas who have limited access to any support services, and those dealing with the loss of working animals who may not conceptualise emotional support as something available to them.

You are not obligated to offer a sliding scale, but you may choose to reserve a small number of discounted spots per month for clients in genuine financial hardship. Typically two to four discounted sessions per month is manageable without significantly affecting your income.

If you offer a sliding scale, be clear about how it works. Ask clients to self-assess against simple criteria rather than asking for financial documentation. A statement like "I offer a limited number of reduced-rate sessions for clients experiencing significant financial hardship. If this applies to you, please mention it when you enquire" is honest and dignified.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Should I charge in NZD or offer pricing in USD since TRACE is based in the US?**
Always charge clients in NZD. Your practice is based in New Zealand and your clients will expect NZD pricing. The USD figure for your TRACE certification is a one-time cost to you, not a pricing model to follow.

**Is NZD $100 to $170 per session really what the market will pay in Auckland?**
Yes. New Zealanders are accustomed to paying in this range for life coaching, wellness consultations, and similar services. The key is presenting your service clearly and professionally so clients understand what they are paying for.

**Can I charge more if I have additional qualifications?**
Yes. If you hold a counselling qualification, a social work background, or relevant life experience that adds credibility, you can position at the upper end or above the established range. The critical thing is to be able to articulate the value you provide.

**Should I include GST in my quoted prices?**
If you are not GST-registered, you cannot charge GST and should not include it. If you are GST-registered, you can either include GST in your advertised price (most consumer-facing services do this in NZ) or add it at invoicing. Including GST in your headline price avoids confusion.

**What if a client can't afford even my lowest rate?**
For genuine hardship cases, a sliding scale or a single free session can be offered as a one-time measure. You are not a welfare service, and you cannot sustain your practice at zero revenue. Signposting clients to other free resources (SPCA welfare services, community support lines) is appropriate when your services are not financially accessible.
