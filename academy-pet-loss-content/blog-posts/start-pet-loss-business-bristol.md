## What Does a Pet Loss Practitioner Do?

A pet loss practitioner provides structured, compassionate one-to-one support to people grieving the death of an animal companion. Sessions are typically 50 to 60 minutes, delivered in person or via video call.

The work is structured conversation, not clinical therapy. A clear framework guides the client through their grief in stages: telling the full story of their animal, recognising the depth and significance of the bond, acknowledging the pain of the loss directly and honestly, celebrating the animal's life and personality, and beginning to understand what it means to carry that relationship forward. The aim is not to hurry grief to its end but to accompany the client through it in a purposeful, structured way.

This is accessible work. It does not require a clinical psychology licence or a registered therapy qualification. Approximately 97% of what a skilled pet loss practitioner does is compassionate presence and structured conversation. People who come to this field from veterinary nursing, life coaching, counselling, social work, or simply from a personal history with animal loss can all develop effective practices. The discipline comes from having a good framework. The TRACE certification provides exactly that.

## Do You Need a Formal Qualification to Practise in Bristol?

There is no statutory regulation in the UK that applies specifically to pet loss practitioners. You are not required to hold a mandatory licence or register with a governing body before offering pet bereavement support.

In Bristol, however, certification matters in a particular way. The city's wellness sector is saturated and professionally competitive. Clients who are already accustomed to engaging with qualified therapists, coaches, and counsellors will apply the same standard to a pet loss practitioner. If you present yourself without a recognised certification, you will struggle to build trust in a market where trust is the primary currency.

Referral partners in the veterinary community, including the many independent practices and the University of Bristol's School of Veterinary Sciences in nearby Langford, will assess your credentials before recommending you. A TRACE certificate from a recognised Academy is the kind of credible, named qualification that opens those conversations.

Existing qualifications in counselling, coaching, nursing, social work, or animal care are a valuable complement, but they are not a prerequisite for entering the pet loss field.

## What to Charge for Pet Loss Support Sessions

Early-career pet loss practitioners in Bristol typically charge between £50 and £80 per session. Given Bristol's relatively high willingness to invest in wellbeing services, rates of £75 to £100 are achievable as your reputation develops, and experienced practitioners in the city can charge more.

Memorial pages are priced at your discretion. Including one as part of a first session package, or offering them as a standalone service at £30 to £60, adds value without significant additional time.

At £70 per session, four clients per week generates £280 per week. Your TRACE certification cost is recovered within five to six weeks of active practice.
