# How to Start a Pet Loss Support Business in Melbourne

Melbourne has a reputation for being one of the most liveable cities in the world, and part of what makes it that is how deeply its residents invest in their relationships, including the ones they share with animals. From the dog-crowded paths of the Tan track to the pet-friendly cafes of Fitzroy and the parks of Brighton, animals are visible and valued here in a way that is woven into the culture of the city. When a pet dies, the grief that follows is real and often severe. The professional support available for it in Melbourne is almost non-existent. That represents both a human problem and a genuine opportunity for the right person.

## The Growing Demand for Pet Loss Support in Melbourne

Australia has one of the highest rates of pet ownership in the world. Around 69% of Australian households own a pet, and Melbourne, as the country's second-largest city, reflects that broadly. The city's culture of outdoor living, laneway culture, community markets, and neighbourhood identity has created a population that forms strong, specific attachments to place and to the animals that are part of their daily experience.

For many Melburnians, particularly those living alone, those who have relocated from other cities or countries, and families who acquired a pet during the pandemic years of remote work, the human-animal bond is the most consistent relationship in their lives. The RSPCA consistently documents that Australians regard their pets as family, and the grief experienced when a pet dies often rivals the grief felt at the loss of a close human relative.

Despite this, specialist support for pet bereavement is almost entirely absent from Melbourne's mental health and wellness landscape. General grief counsellors rarely have specific training in animal loss. Veterinary practices do their best at the time of euthanasia but can offer little in the weeks and months that follow. The gap is large, the need is real, and very few trained practitioners exist to fill it.

## What Does a Pet Loss Practitioner Do?

A pet loss practitioner provides structured, compassionate support to people grieving the death of an animal companion. This is not clinical psychotherapy. You are not diagnosing or treating a mental health condition, and your services are not within the Medicare or NDIS frameworks. What you offer is a professionally guided, non-clinical space where a person's loss is taken seriously, their bond with their animal is honoured, and their grief is worked through using a clear and proven method.

In a typical session, you guide a client through a structured process: exploring the story of the animal's life and death, understanding the specific nature of the relationship, acknowledging the pain of the loss without minimising it, celebrating what the animal meant, and helping the client find a way to carry that bond forward. Sessions are usually one hour, conducted in person or by video call, and can unfold over several weeks depending on the client's needs and the complexity of the loss.

This work sits in the same professional space as life coaching, peer support, and wellness facilitation. It is meaningful, skilled, and in genuine demand. It simply requires the right training to deliver it well.

## Do You Need a Licence or Certification in Victoria?

Pet loss support at the peer level does not require AHPRA registration or any clinical qualification in Victoria or anywhere else in Australia. Clinical health professions regulated by AHPRA include psychology, social work, and certain counselling roles. Peer-level emotional support for pet bereavement falls entirely outside that framework because it is not clinical treatment.

This means you can begin practising as a pet loss practitioner in Melbourne once you have completed appropriate training, without applying to a registration body or professional association. That said, professional certification is essential for building a credible, referral-supported practice. Veterinary clinics, specialist animal hospitals, and pet cremation services in Melbourne will want to know about your training before referring clients to you. Potential clients searching for support online will look for qualifications before booking.

Getting properly certified is both the right thing to do and the practical foundation of a sustainable business. It tells every person who encounters your practice that your work is grounded in a recognised framework, not just in goodwill.

## The TRACE Certification: Your Route Into Practice

The TRACE programme, offered through the Academy for Pet Loss at academyforpetloss.com, is built specifically for people who want to provide professional pet bereavement support. TRACE stands for Therapeutic Remembrance for Animal Companions and their Endings. The framework follows five phases: Tell the Story, Recognise the Bond, Acknowledge the Pain, Celebrate the Life, and Embrace What Remains.

The programme is grounded in established grief research. Worden's Four Tasks of Mourning and Continuing Bonds Theory both inform the TRACE model, giving your practice a solid theoretical base. The application, however, is deliberately non-clinical. Approximately 97% of what you do as a TRACE practitioner is compassionate presence and structured conversation. ## Building Your Client Base in Melbourne

Melbourne rewards practitioners who invest in relationships. The city's strong neighbourhood identities, its culture of community engagement, and its dense professional networks make referral-building particularly effective if you approach it in the right way.

Veterinary practices are your most important early referral partners. Melbourne has a large and varied vet landscape, from boutique independent clinics in Fitzroy, Carlton, Richmond, and Prahran, to specialist hospitals in the inner suburbs. Target independent practices first. Introduce yourself to the practice manager, explain what you offer, and leave a clear one-page overview and business card. Frame the conversation around how you can support their clients after they leave the clinic. Many vets would genuinely welcome that option.

Pet cremation and memorial services are a close second. Businesses in this space deal with families at the peak of their grief every single week, and a trusted referral to a trained practitioner is something many of them would be glad to be able to offer. A single visit and a warm introduction can open a long-term referral relationship.

Melbourne's café culture and community events scene also present informal networking opportunities that other cities do not. Dog-friendly markets, pet expos, and local community groups are all spaces where you can build genuine connections before any formal marketing is needed.

## Key Referral Partners to Approach in Melbourne

### Veterinary Clinics and Animal Hospitals
Independent practices in high-pet-density neighbourhoods like Fitzroy, Collingwood, South Yarra, and Hawthorn are the best starting point. University of Melbourne Veterinary Hospital and specialist centres like the Animal Referral Hospital (ARH) are also worth approaching once you have established yourself.

### Pet Cremation and Memorial Services
Melbourne has several dedicated pet cremation services. These businesses interact with families in acute grief on a daily basis and can become a sustained referral source after a single professional introduction.

### RSPCA Victoria and Local Rescue Organisations
RSPCA Victoria operates widely across the state and its staff experience animal loss regularly. Breed-specific rescues and community foster networks are also worth engaging as sources of referral and community presence.

### Groomers, Boarding Kennels, and Dog Day Care Businesses
Long-term client relationships mean these providers often know about a pet's death before almost anyone outside the family. A business card and a brief conversation is often enough to plant a referral relationship.

### Holistic and Wellness Practitioners
Melbourne has a particularly strong wellness and complementary health community. Naturopaths, yoga teachers, and holistic therapists often encounter clients carrying unprocessed grief. A cross-referral relationship with practitioners in this space can be mutually beneficial.

## What to Charge

Pet loss support sits outside the Medicare and NDIS frameworks, which means you set your own rates without reference to government schedules. Peer-level support practitioners in Australia typically charge between AUD $100 and AUD $180 per session. Melbourne's cost of living and the relative affluence of many pet-owning households in the inner suburbs support rates in the middle to upper part of that range.

Session packages of three or five sessions offered at a modest discount encourage client commitment and provide more predictable income. Memorial pages, which you receive ten of on completing either TRACE programme, can be offered as a standalone service or included in a package. Many practitioners charge between AUD $80 and AUD $200 per memorial page.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to be registered with AHPRA to offer pet loss support in Melbourne?
No. Pet loss support at the peer level is not a clinical service and does not require AHPRA registration. You are not providing psychological treatment or regulated counselling. The TRACE certification is the training credential you need to practise responsibly.

### Is this service covered by Medicare or private health insurance?
No. Pet bereavement support is not covered by Medicare or private health insurance, as it is not a clinical service. Clients pay directly, which simplifies your billing and administration significantly.

### Can I operate this practice alongside existing work in health, wellness, or education?
Yes, and many practitioners do exactly that. The self-paced TRACE programme is designed to fit around other commitments, and the practice itself can be run part-time from home, scaling up as your client base and referral network grow.

### How does Melbourne's market compare to other Australian cities?
Melbourne's strong community culture, its high density of independent veterinary practices, and its well-established wellness sector make it a particularly good environment for building a referral-based pet loss practice. The city rewards practitioners who invest in genuine relationships.

### What if I want to specialise, for example in equine loss or the grief of shelter workers?
The TRACE framework can be applied to any form of animal loss. The Extended Programme's additional modules provide depth for complex and specialist situations. Melbourne has a significant equestrian community in and around the outer suburbs, which represents a niche with real unmet need.
