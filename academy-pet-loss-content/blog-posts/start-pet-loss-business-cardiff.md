## What Does a Pet Loss Practitioner Do?

Pet loss practitioners do not provide clinical therapy. They are not diagnosing mental health conditions or managing clinical presentations of grief disorder. What they do is sit with people who are struggling in the aftermath of losing an animal they loved, and guide them through a structured process that helps them move forward without feeling rushed, dismissed, or alone.

Sessions typically run 50 to 60 minutes. You help the client tell the full story of their pet: how they came into their life, what they were like, what the daily rituals looked like, and what the final chapter involved. You help the client name and recognise the specific bond they shared. You work through the pain directly, rather than trying to bypass or minimise it. You help the client celebrate what their pet's life meant. And you help them begin to integrate the loss into their ongoing identity, finding what remains in their life after the grief.

Some clients need two or three sessions to feel complete. Others come back for six or more. The work is flexible, meaningful, and deeply personal.

## Do You Need a Formal Qualification to Practise in Cardiff?

Pet loss counselling is not a statutory regulated profession in Wales or anywhere in the UK. There is no legal requirement to hold a specific qualification or registration to offer pet loss support services. Technically, anyone can offer it.

But technical legality and good professional practice are different things. Clients who are grieving deserve a trained practitioner. Vets, cremation services, and community organisations in Cardiff will expect to see credible certification before they refer clients to you. Professional certification is also what enables you to charge a fair and appropriate fee, to join quality directories, and to present yourself to corporate referral partners with authority.

If you already work in veterinary nursing, counselling, coaching, social work, teaching, or a related field, your background is an asset. If you are coming from an entirely different career, or from personal experience of pet loss with no formal training at all, a well-designed certification programme provides everything you need to practise confidently, ethically, and effectively.

## The TRACE Certification: Your Route Into Practice

The TRACE certification is offered by the Academy for Pet Loss and is built specifically for people who want to practise pet loss support professionally. TRACE stands for Therapeutic Remembrance for Animal Companions and their Endings.

The framework that sits at the heart of every TRACE session has five steps: Tell the Story, Recognise the Bond, Acknowledge the Pain, Celebrate the Life, and Embrace What Remains. Each step is purposeful, sequenced, and grounded in established grief theory. The TRACE model draws on Worden's Four Tasks of Mourning and Continuing Bonds Theory, but applies them in a non-clinical way that is accessible to practitioners without a therapy background.
