As that understanding becomes more widely held, Glaswegians who grieve their pets are less willing to be told to simply "get over it" and more actively searching for qualified support. A TRACE-certified practitioner who is local, visible, and approachable stands to meet that demand directly.

## What Does a Pet Loss Practitioner Do?

Pet loss practitioners provide structured, compassionate support to people grieving the death of an animal companion. The work is not clinical therapy. You are not diagnosing conditions, prescribing treatments, or managing mental health crises. What you are doing is accompanying someone through one of life's genuine losses, with a framework that gives both of you direction.

In practice, sessions typically run 50 to 60 minutes, held online or in person. You help your client tell the full story of their pet. You acknowledge the specific nature of the bond they shared, the routines, the rituals, the relationship that made that animal irreplaceable. You work through the pain without minimising it. You help the client celebrate what their pet's life meant. And you help them begin to integrate the loss, to find what remains in their life beyond the grief.

Some clients need two or three sessions. Others benefit from a series of six or more. You set your own hours, work from wherever suits you, and build the practice at a pace that fits your life.

## Do You Need a Formal Qualification to Practise in Glasgow?

Pet loss counselling is not a statutory regulated profession in the UK or Scotland. There is no law that requires a specific qualification to offer pet loss support. In that sense, it is an open field.

But openness does not mean anything goes. Grieving clients are vulnerable, and the practitioners they trust should be properly trained. Veterinary practices, pet cremation services, and other referral partners in Glasgow will expect to see credible certification before they point their clients in your direction. Professional certification also positions you to charge appropriate fees, appear in reputable directories, and be taken seriously by larger referral sources such as pet insurance companies.

If your background is in veterinary nursing, counselling, coaching, social work, or teaching, your existing skills transfer naturally into pet loss support. If you are coming from an entirely different background, or from personal experience of pet loss rather than professional experience, the right certification programme provides the complete framework you need.

The TRACE certification, offered by the Academy for Pet Loss, is built specifically for pet loss practitioners. TRACE stands for Therapeutic Remembrance for Animal Companions and their Endings.

The five-step framework is the engine of every session: Tell the Story, Recognise the Bond, Acknowledge the Pain, Celebrate the Life, and Embrace What Remains. Each step is purposeful, sequenced, and grounded in established grief theory, including Worden's Four Tasks of Mourning and Continuing Bonds Theory. The framework is non-clinical by design.
