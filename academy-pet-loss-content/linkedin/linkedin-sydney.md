Around 69% of Australian households own a pet. Almost none of them have access to trained, specialist support when that animal dies.

Sydney is the country's largest city, and the gap between pet owners who are grieving and practitioners who can help them is almost entirely unfilled.

Pet loss counselling is one of the most underserved niches in the Australian wellness space. Grieving pet owners search for help online, call their vet, and reach out to friends. What they rarely find is a trained professional who takes their loss seriously and knows how to help.

What a pet loss practice in Sydney looks like:

- Structured one-to-one sessions, online or in person, guided by the TRACE framework
- No AHPRA registration required. Pet loss support is peer-level, not clinical, and sits outside Medicare and NDIS
- Referral partnerships with Sydney's veterinary clinics, pet cremation services, the RSPCA, and local shelters
- Session rates typically AUD $100-$180 for peer support practitioners, with memorial pages as an add-on service
