Melbourne has a deep, visible culture of caring for animals. It also has almost no specialist support for people when those animals die.

That is a real gap, and it is growing.

Pet loss grief is one of the most underestimated and underserved forms of bereavement. Melburnians form close, lasting bonds with their animals. When a pet dies, the grief is often severe and the professional support available is nearly absent. A trained practitioner who takes this work seriously fills a need that is not being met anywhere else in the market.

What building a pet loss practice in Melbourne looks like:

- One-to-one sessions, in person or online, using a structured, evidence-grounded framework
- No AHPRA registration required. This is non-clinical peer support, outside Medicare and NDIS
- Referral relationships with Melbourne's independent vet clinics, specialist animal hospitals, cremation services, and the RSPCA
- Session rates typically AUD $100-$180, with session packages and memorial pages as added revenue

The TRACE Practitioner Certification from the Academy for Pet Loss trains you in the TRACE method and provides your practitioner certificate, a directory listing, and ten memorial pages to offer clients. The Core Programme is $395 USD and self-paced. The Extended Programme, which adds Complex Loss and Widening the Circle modules, is $525 USD.

Melbourne's community culture, its strong neighbourhood identity, and its well-established wellness sector make it one of the best environments in Australia to build a referral-based practice in this space.
