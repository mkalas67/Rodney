# LinkedIn Post: Birmingham

51% of UK households own a pet. Birmingham has over a million residents. The number of certified pet loss counsellors serving this city is negligible.

When a family in Solihull, Harborne, or Kings Heath loses a dog they have had for twelve years, they are navigating grief without a professional to turn to. Their vet cannot help. A general grief counsellor is not trained for it. The bereavement charities are not set up for it.

That is not a small gap. It is a substantial unmet need in one of the UK's most populated cities.

The TRACE certification from the Academy for Pet Loss is designed for people who want to fill that gap professionally. It works for:

- Vet nurses and veterinary receptionists who see this grief every day
- Life coaches and therapists looking for a meaningful specialism
- Human counsellors who want to extend their offering
- Career changers who have experienced pet loss and want to help others

The programme provides a five-step evidence-based framework, a TRACE Practitioner Certificate, a premium directory listing, and memorial pages to offer to clients. It is self-paced, video-based, and does not require a clinical qualification.

Birmingham's veterinary community, its pet cremation services, and its diverse, tight-knit neighbourhoods are all waiting for practitioners to step into this space.
