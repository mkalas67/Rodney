Pet loss is the most common form of bereavement in the UK. Roughly half of all households own a pet, and yet the death of an animal is still treated as a minor inconvenience by most of our wellbeing infrastructure.

In Leeds, there are dozens of human bereavement counsellors. There are almost no qualified pet loss practitioners.

That gap is significant. Research shows that losing a pet can trigger a grief response as intense as losing a close family member. People in Leeds are experiencing that grief right now, with no trained support available locally.

- On qualifying, you receive a TRACE Practitioner Certificate, a directory listing, and ten memorial pages to offer clients
- No clinical background required. The TRACE framework is built for compassionate, structured conversation, accessible to practitioners from any background

Leeds is underserved. The demand is real. And the first qualified pet loss practitioner to build a visible practice here will have a significant head start.
