Seattle has one of the highest rates of pet ownership in the US, and almost no professional support for people when those pets die.

That gap is an opportunity.

Pet loss is one of the most underserved areas in the wellness and counseling space. People who lose a dog or cat often describe the grief as some of the most intense they have ever felt. Yet there is almost nowhere for them to turn.

Here is what a pet loss practice in Seattle actually looks like:

- One-to-one sessions, in person or online, guiding clients through structured grief support
- No clinical license required. This is peer-level support, not therapy
- Referral partnerships with vet clinics, cremation services, and animal shelters
- A starting rate of $100-$125 per session, with packages and memorial add-ons available

The TRACE Practitioner Certification from the Academy for Pet Loss gives you the framework, the credentials, and the directory listing to launch. The Core Programme is $395 and is self-paced. You can be seeing clients within weeks of completing it.

Seattle's tech culture, its outdoor dog community, and its openness to wellness services make it one of the best cities in the country to build this kind of practice.
