# City Localisation Data — Academy for Pet Loss

All pricing ranges are for pet bereavement support sessions (not clinical therapy).
Early = first 1-2 years in practice. Established = 3+ years with referral network.

---

## LONDON, UK
- **Currency:** GBP
- **Spelling:** UK English
- **Session pricing:** £70-£120 (early), £90-£160 (established)
- **Business registration:** Register as sole trader with HMRC (self-assessment), or form a limited company via Companies House. Most practitioners start as sole traders.
- **Professional insurance:** Relevant bodies include BACP (British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy) — note TRACE practitioners are not clinical counsellors but professional indemnity insurance is strongly recommended. Balens and Towergate offer policies for wellbeing practitioners.
- **Key referral sources:** Vets4Pets (national chain), Medivet (many London branches), independent practices in Hampstead, Islington, Richmond. Blue Cross, Cats Protection, PDSA, Dogs Trust Harefield.
- **Pet cremation services:** London Pet Crematorium (Herefordshire), local mobile cremation services operating into Greater London. Aquamation gaining traction.
- **Local directories:** Counselling Directory, Bark.com, Not A Dry Eye (pet loss specific UK directory), Psychology Today UK.
- **Social platforms:** Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn. TikTok growing among under-35 pet owners.
- **Cultural notes:** High density of flat-dwelling pet owners. Post-COVID adoption spike created a large cohort of first-time pet owners now facing first pet losses. Diverse multilingual population in many boroughs. Clients comfortable with video sessions. Expect professional presentation.
- **Local online communities:** Multiple London-based pet loss Facebook groups. Nextdoor active in neighbourhoods.
- **Regulatory context:** No statutory regulation of pet loss practitioners in England. Not required to register with BACP, UKCP, or other bodies — but professional membership adds credibility when approaching vet practices.

## DUBLIN, IRELAND
- **Currency:** EUR
- **Spelling:** UK/Irish English (use "counselling", "practitioner", etc.)
- **Session pricing:** €65-€110 (early), €85-€140 (established)
- **Business registration:** Register as a sole trader with Revenue Commissioners Ireland (no formal registration required in Ireland for sole traders unless trading under a business name). Rural-to-urban migration means many Dublin residents have strong connections to farming animals as well as companion pets.
- **Regulatory context:** Ireland. Pet loss support is not regulated under the Health and Social Care Professionals Act 2005 (which covers professions like occupational therapy, social work, etc.). No IACP (Irish Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy) registration required — but membership helps with professional credibility and referrals.
- **Social platforms:** Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn especially active among Dublin professionals.

## NEW YORK CITY, USA
- **Currency:** USD
- **Spelling:** US English
- **Session pricing:** $150-$250 (early), $200-$300+ (established) — NYC carries a significant premium
- **Business registration:** File as a sole proprietor (no formal registration needed) or form an LLC via the New York Division of Corporations. If operating under a business name, file a DBA (Doing Business As). Pet loss support is not clinical therapy and does not require a New York State therapy license.
- **Key referral sources:** ASPCA (headquartered in NYC), Animal Medical Center (Upper East Side), NYC veterinary clinics (particularly Upper East Side, Upper West Side, Brooklyn neighbourhoods), Hartsdale Pet Cemetery (Westchester, first pet cemetery in the US — strong referral potential).
- **Pet cremation services:** Faithful Friends Pet Cremation, NY Pet Memorial.
- **Local directories:** Psychology Today, GoodTherapy, Thumbtack, Bark.com, local NYC pet loss support group listings.
- **Cultural notes:** New Yorkers are sophisticated consumers. Professionalism and credentialing matter. The market is large enough to sustain a full practice. Many clients will prefer video sessions given NYC commute culture. Apartment pets (especially dogs) are primary companions for many residents. Pet loss grief is widely taken seriously. Strong willingness to pay for quality support.
- **Social platforms:** Instagram, LinkedIn (very active professional market), Facebook groups by neighbourhood.
- **Regulatory context:** New York State does not license "pet loss counselors" separately. The key distinction is between clinical mental health counseling (requires LMHC licensure in NY) and supportive/educational pet loss services (no license required). Market as "certified pet loss practitioner" rather than "counselor" to be safe.

- **Regulatory context:** Australia. Pet loss support does not fall under AHPRA-regulated professions (medicine, psychology, social work). The title "counsellor" is largely unregulated in Australia (unlike psychologist or psychotherapist). Use "certified pet loss practitioner" for clarity.
